Generated Curriculum: UAE Contract Law

Course Overview

This comprehensive 14-week course on UAE Contract Law explores the fundamental principles and practical applications of contract law in the United Arab Emirates. Students will examine the Civil Transactions Law (Federal Law No. 5 of 1985) and related legislation, analyze key court decisions, and develop practical skills in drafting and analyzing contracts under UAE law.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Analyze the essential elements of a valid contract under UAE law
  2. Evaluate different types of contracts and their legal implications in the UAE legal system
  3. Apply legal principles to determine remedies for breach of contract
  4. Draft contract clauses that comply with UAE legal requirements
  5. Compare UAE contract law with international contract principles and practices
  6. Analyze recent court decisions affecting contract interpretation and enforcement in the UAE

Module Structure

Module 1: Foundations of UAE Contract Law (Weeks 1-2)
  • Introduction to UAE legal system and sources of contract law
  • The Civil Transactions Law (Federal Law No. 5 of 1985)
  • Influence of Sharia principles on contract law
  • Comparing UAE contract law with common law approaches
Module 2: Contract Formation (Weeks 3-4)
  • Essential elements of a valid contract
  • Offer and acceptance
  • Capacity to contract
  • Consideration and cause
  • Form requirements and formalities
Module 3: Contract Interpretation and Performance (Weeks 5-6)
  • Principles of contract interpretation
  • Implied terms and good faith requirements
  • Performance obligations
  • Case analysis: Dubai Court of Cassation Case No. 146/2018

Assessment Methods

  • Midterm Examination (25%): In-class examination covering Modules 1-3
  • Contract Drafting Assignment (20%): Draft a commercial contract that complies with UAE legal requirements
  • Case Analysis Presentation (15%): Analysis and presentation of a significant UAE contract law case
  • Research Paper (15%): 2,500-word paper on an approved contract law topic
  • Final Examination (25%): Comprehensive examination covering all course material
