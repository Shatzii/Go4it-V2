2 · Dual-Language Immersion Framework
Train in German · Learn in English · Live in Both

"TRAIN IN GERMAN. LEARN IN ENGLISH. LIVE IN BOTH."

The core innovation of Direction B is simple: language is not a separate class — it is woven into training, daily life, and reflection. Every athlete becomes a dual-language learner through authentic use, not worksheets.

U.S. Students Earn

  • German Language Immersion A1–A2 (1.0 Cr) — German embedded in training, meals, and excursions.
  • English Literacy Through Performance (1.0 Cr) — reading, writing, and communication built around sport and leadership.

International Students Earn

  • English Language Development (1.0 Cr) — academic and athletic English for global opportunities.
  • German Immersion Credit (1.0 Cr) — practical German for life, school, and sport in Austria/EU.

Day Program Students Choose

  • Option A: German Immersion + English Literacy.
  • Option B: Advanced German + Specialized Electives.
  • Schedule adapted to fit local schooling while maintaining rigor.

4 · Residency Program · 12-Credit Catalog
Course Codes · Credits · Descriptions

Language Core · 2.0 Credits

WL-401: German Language Immersion A1–A2 (1.0 Cr)

German is integrated directly into training sessions, meals, transportation, and cultural excursions. Approximately 30% of athletic instruction occurs in German, with progressive complexity and practical focus.

  • Outcomes: A1–A2 proficiency, sport-specific vocabulary, real-world task confidence.
  • Evidence: Spoken command responses, task completion videos, vocabulary tracking.

ELA-401: Performance Communication (1.0 Cr) · U.S. Students

English reading, writing, and speaking built around performance — film breakdowns, leadership scenarios, and reflective analysis of training and competition.

ELA-402: English Language Development (1.0 Cr) · International Students

For multilingual students needing academic and athletic English development. Emphasis on practical, sport-anchored communication and academic literacy.

Science & Analytics Core · 3.0 Credits

SCI-401: Human Performance Biology (1.0 Cr)

Training sessions become living laboratories. Students learn anatomy, physiology, and biomechanics through their own movement, testing, and performance.

MATH-401: Applied Sports Analytics (1.0 Cr)

Performance data (speed, power, GPS metrics, game stats) becomes the raw material for studying algebra, functions, statistics, and data visualization.

HE-401: Nutrition & Recovery Science (1.0 Cr)

Sleep, hydration, fueling, and recovery tracked and analyzed like a science project — with real consequences for daily performance.

Global Studies Core · 3.0 Credits

SOC-401: Global Sports Sociology (1.0 Cr)

Compares European and American sports systems: culture, community, economics, and identity.

HIST-401: Austrian & EU Systems (1.0 Cr)

Uses Vienna and Central Europe as primary sources. Site visits and cultural labs become history class.

CS-401: Digital Portfolio & Data Literacy (1.0 Cr)

Students learn to treat their own development as a dataset — building a digital portfolio that integrates film, metrics, reflections, and artifacts.

Experiential Portfolio · 4.0 Credits

EXP-401: Cultural Immersion Portfolio (1.0 Cr)

Language and cultural evidence across 12 weeks, curated into a coherent narrative.

EXP-402: Training-as-Science Practicum (1.0 Cr)

Every training session treated as an experiment: plan, test, measure, adjust, reflect.

EXP-403: Leadership & Community Engagement (1.0 Cr)

Real projects that serve teammates, host communities, and younger athletes.

EXP-404: Global Scholar-Athlete Capstone (1.0 Cr)

Final synthesis showing growth as a scholar, athlete, and global citizen — in two languages where possible.
